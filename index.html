Zapp Station is an EV charging station finder app.

EV drivers were experiencing constant anxiety about charging availability. Research with 15+ users revealed the core issue:unreliable information leading to broken trustin the charging infrastructure. So Zapp Station is introduced to help riders for easily accessing the chargers and plan the long rides care free.

My Role

I led the project as both a Brand Designer and Product Designer.
Brand Identity / Logo Design

EV is the replacement for carbon emission vehicles or engines so it signifies the preservation of nature thats the preservation of greenery, so I have the idea in color by sticking to the mother nature color which is light green signs of green energy and friendly as well. Then for the type I created the custom logo font just fot Zapp and created in a slightly tilted manner with round minimal style signifies technology and motion of evolution. Also created the Logo mark or we call it Monogram, which is representing the landmark with Z on it and also a leaf like structure to dedicated greenery of environment.

Product UI/UX Designer

A human-first UI with soft interactions and focused flows Built-in design system to ensure consistency across screens Clean, distraction-free interface to help users act quickly and confidently Tested for mobile, smartwatch, and car dashboard environments

Challenges

No reliable charger info:

EV drivers often reach stations that are offline, full, or out of service.

No real-time updates:

Users have no live status they cant tell if a charger is working or available.

No trip planning around charging:

Long trips are stressful without knowing where and when to charge.

Missing station details:

Most apps dont show charger types, photos, or nearby amenities.

No tool for station owners:

Merchants couldnt easily list or update station info, leading to outdated data.

Solutions

Real-time map

Integration real time map of EV stations with live charger status.

Trip Planner

Introduced the trip planner according with charging stops along a selected route.

Vehicle onboarding

Add you EVs with dynamic, engaging UI so that the app can recognize your ev and set accordingly.

Solutions

Advanced filter

Addition of filter by charger type, distance, amenities and nearby location, favorites etc.

Subscription

Subscription plan has added so that for busy schedule there will be no delay and hassel free charging based charging access.

Merchant UI

We added the merchant side app interface as well for station owner so that they can update station info, photos, and amenities.

Process

Research & Discovery

User interviews, ride-alongs with taxi drivers, competitive analysis, and pain point mapping.

Solution Architecture

Feature prioritization, user flow design, and cross-platform experience mapping.

UI, Interaction and Design System

Intuitive interface for customers and station owners along with Component library creation with 50+ elements ensuring consistency across iOS, Android, and CarPlay.

Different interaction for different actions are created to maintain lively hood of the app and maintained the interaction consistency along with vehicle onboarding.

Testing & Iteration

sability testing, A/B testing of critical flows, and beta testing with target user group

Outcome

  • Zapp Station reduces charging anxiety and saves EV users time by providing clear, real-time, and reliable charging information.
  • Long-distance travel becomes easier and stress-free with the Trip Planner feature.
  • Merchants benefit by attracting users with verified, up-to-date station data creating a smarter EV ecosystem overall.

Connecting Health & Technology Through Intuitive Design

MaxiHealth is a modern web platform offering curated health service packages to individuals and organizations. It connects users with top healthcare professionals allowing them to access plans and services from the comfort of their home. MaxiHealth also partners with companies to provide employee health packages, making health benefits more accessible and transparent through a simple, reliable website.

Challenges

Trust and reliability in design

Health platforms must look and feel credible. The UI needed to reflect both professionalism and approachability.

Cluttered information flow

Users were unsure what MaxiHealth offered and how it worked. The goal was to simplify the flow and make it easy to compare plans.

Dry and cold visual tone

Health sites are often dull or overly corporate. We wanted to add a friendly and human touch with illustrations and soft visuals.

Complex plan selection

Choosing the right package can be overwhelming. We designed clear CTAs, intuitive forms, and playful guidance to help users decide.

Video and education integration

Explaining how MaxiHealth works was key we added video sections and visual storytelling to engage and inform users.

Process

I led the UI design and crafted a clean, minimal visual system with a human touch. I also designed custom illustrations to balance seriousness with warmth making the experience friendly and reliable.

  • Designed the responsive website layout, forms, pricing UI, and package flows
  • Created a soft, illustration-based visual language
  • Curated responsive UI in Figma for all viewports
  • Worked closely with the product team to refine messaging, trust cues, and info hierarchy

Outcome

MaxiHealth now presents itself as a reliable, modern health brand. Users feel confident exploring and purchasing health packages online. The illustration-led design gives it personality, while the clean UI ensures usability and trust key in the health industry.

Web3 platform where players can play tournament from different wallet or network.

Multichain Tournament is a Web3-based gaming platform that brings turn-based games like Gangwars into a structured, competitive ecosystem. The platform allows players to join tournaments, manage team profiles, view match history, and climb leaderboards all while using a seamless wallet or zk-login onboarding system. Its built for competitive players and Web3 gamers looking for smooth tournament participation, decentralized player identity, and team-based gameplay.

Challenges

Complex onboarding with Web3 logins

Players had to connect via crypto wallets or zk login. The challenge was to make this smooth and beginner-friendly.

Real-time match scheduling clarity

Designing an intuitive experience for players to view schedules, join matches, and get real-time updates was key.

Bracket structure design

Brackets are core to the experience. I custom-designed a clear, scrollable bracket UI with interactive hover states and team mapping.

Player and team profile clarity

Gamers needed personalized spaces showing their identity, match history, stats, and their team's data all within a compact and readable layout.

Game info without overwhelming users

Presenting the concept of a multichain tournament platform, blockchain tech, and game mechanics without overwhelming new users was a balance of UX writing and UI flow.

Process

As the UI/UX Designer, I shaped the products core flows and interaction model. From wallet connection to match join flow, my goal was to keep things fast, clear, and exciting for competitive players.

  • Designed the onboarding flow (wallet + zk-login)
  • Created custom bracket layouts for mobile and desktop
  • Designed leaderboards, profile views, and match interfaces
  • Focused on micro-interactions and feedback to keep players engaged
  • Structured complex Web3 interactions into simple steps

Outcome

Multichain Tournament transforms how players interact with Web3 games. The result is a clear, engaging platform where players can join matches effortlessly, track performance, and connect with teams all through a UI designed to reduce friction and enhance competitive energy.

A web3 pixel war where Factions Fight for Territory.

The Battle of Ruby Valley is a Web3-based pixel art NFT game where players are assigned to factions and battle for territory in a dynamic, expanding valley map. Players earn rewards, stake tokens, and conquer regions through strategic attacks and faction wars.

Built on blockchain, the game blends NFT mechanics with territorial gameplay and pixel art aesthetics, offering immersive community-driven battles powered by real token staking and earning.

Challenges

Web3 onboarding complexity

Wallet connection and player onboarding needed to be simple, gamified, and intuitive, especially for new Web3 users.

Dynamic war flows

I designed the entire flow from entering a war, attacking, and ending the battle with clear feedback loops and engaging visuals.

Faction assignment and gameplay clarity

Players are divided into factions. I designed faction summary UIs, profile views, and status screens that are both thematic and easy to navigate.

Challenges

Pixel art asset integration

I created original pixel art assets consistent with the games lore and mechanics from map icons to character/faction illustrations.

Token mechanics

The game involves staking and swapping tokens. I designed the in-game token swap interface, ensuring secure and seamless interactions.

Process

As the UI/UX Designer, I shaped the products core flows and interaction model. From wallet connection to match join flow, my goal was to keep things fast, clear, and exciting for competitive players.

  • Designed the UI/UX for onboarding, battles, staking, and profiles
  • Crafted pixel art game assets and icons for maps, factions, and badges
  • Created micro-interactions and animations to enhance the feel of war progression
  • Mapped out Web3 flows (wallet connect, staking, claim rewards) in an engaging, game-first way
  • Delivered a consistent design system combining pixel visuals with Web3 UI patterns

Outcome

The Battle of Ruby Valley now stands as a pixel-perfect, Web3-native game thats both immersive and user-friendly. The onboarding and staking flows lower the barrier for new users, while custom UI and pixel visuals keep the experience playful yet powerful.
